About the role

Folio Labs is building the infrastructure layer for the next generation of creative collaboration tools. As a Senior Platform Engineer you will own the design, reliability, and evolution of our core compute and data platform — the infrastructure that thousands of teams depend on every day.

This is an individual contributor role with significant influence. You will work closely with the engineering leadership team and embed with product squads to design systems that scale predictably. We are a company that ships fast, but we do not compromise on engineering quality: our on-call burden is low, our postmortems are blameless, and we invest heavily in platform observability.

What you will do

  • Design and build the distributed infrastructure that underpins Folio's multi-tenant compute layer, handling over 400,000 requests per minute at peak
  • Own incident response and drive the reliability programme — SLO design, alerting strategy, chaos engineering
  • Partner with product engineering squads to review system designs and provide architectural guidance
  • Build internal developer tooling that reduces friction — local dev environments, CI pipelines, deployment abstractions
  • Contribute meaningfully to the platform roadmap; your technical judgement shapes what we build next

What we are looking for

  • 5+ years working on production distributed systems or platform infrastructure, ideally at scale
  • Deep familiarity with Kubernetes — you understand the scheduler, the networking model, and you have debugged node pressure issues in production
  • Strong TypeScript or Go; experience with Rust is a genuine bonus, not a must-have
  • Comfort operating in AWS: you know when to use managed services and when to roll your own
  • A bias for instrumentation — you believe that if something is not measured, it does not exist
  • Clear written communication; asynchronous by default

What we offer

  • £80,000 – £110,000 depending on experience
  • 0.1 – 0.3% EMI options on a standard 4-year vest, 1-year cliff
  • Remote-first with optional London office access; travel budget for quarterly team offsites
  • £2,000 annual learning budget — conferences, books, courses
  • 25 days holiday + bank holidays + your birthday off
  • Private medical (Vitality) + dental
